## Live Coverage of Men's NCAA Tournament: From March Madness to The Elite Eight!

Close 

 When you’re a college basketball fan, the NCAA men’s basketball tournament is a magical time of the year. Although most people watch the games on television, listening to the action while you're on the road is easier than ever thanks to the multitude of websites, sports[ apps](https://www.lifewire.com/what-are-apps-1616114) , and radio stations with March Madness coverage.

##  Find Local Radio Stations With March Madness Coverage 

 Local radio stations that follow home teams almost always provide audio feeds of those games throughout the season. You can also get broadcast info at [ Westwood One Sports](http://westwoodonesports.com/) , the radio home of the NFL, NCAA football, NCAA basketball, and the men’s and women’s NCAA basketball tournaments.

 Westwood One streams every NCAA basketball game being broadcast and doesn't charge you to listen. You can check out the[ broadcast schedule](https://westwoodonesports.com/?league-archive=2019-20-ncaa-basketball) , then choose a state and program to find your[ local stations](http://westwoodonesports.com/stations/) .

##  Listen to NCAA Basketball Games Online 

 The internet has the most options for following March Madness:

* [ TuneIn](https://tunein.com/) : TuneIn also offers original, live, and on-demand radio all in one spot.[ The College Basketball page](https://tunein.com/radio/College-Basketball-c481303/) lists all the games being played in addition to dozens of radio stations providing live coverage, commentary, and other sports news.
* [ ESPN Radio](http://www.espn.com/espnradio/) : The ESPN Radio site lists daily schedules of all their shows including broadcasts of upcoming games. Best of all, there is no cost to listen. The same goes for listening to games on[ CBS Radio](https://cbssportsradio.radio.com/) .
* [ Dar.FM Radio](http://www.dar.fm/top%5Ftalk.php?user%5Fsearch=NCAA&sub=2&search%5Fterm%5Ftop=Sports) : This site also offers a list of stations broadcasting the games. Paid subscribers can record broadcasts or listen to them live.

##  Men's NCAA Basketball on Satellite Radio 

[ Sirius XM](https://www.siriusxm.com/channels/siriusxm-college-sports-radio) provides a list of every game being played along with the stations broadcasting those games for subscribers on the radio and online.

 You can even set reminders through the Sirius service so that you'll never miss a game. You do have to be a Sirius XM subscriber, which means answering a quick set of questions about your service.

##  March Madness on Your Phone 

 There are plenty of sports apps offering March Madness coverage:

* **TuneIn app** : The[ TuneIn app](https://tunein.com/get-tunein/) gives you access to hundreds of radio stations including those carrying the games. The app is available for[ Android](https://www.lifewire.com/what-is-google-android-1616887) , Windows Phones, Amazon Fire tablets, and certain smart televisions.
* **NCAA March Madness Live App** : If you have an[ iPhone](https://www.lifewire.com/pick-best-iphone-for-you-1999293) , iPad,[ Apple Watch](https://www.lifewire.com/apple-watch-models-4175232) , or[ Apple TV](https://www.lifewire.com/what-is-apple-tv-1994193) , you can listen to all 67 games broadcast across TBS, CBS, TNT, and truTV with the free[ iOS NCAA March Madness Live app](https://apps.apple.com/app/ncaa-march-madness-live-mens-college-basketball/id423246594) . Android users can snag the[ NCAA March Madness app through Google Play](https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=com.ncaa.mmlive.app) . Amazon also offers a[ March Madness app for Fire Tablets](https://www.amazon.com/Turner-Sports-Interactive-Inc-Madness/dp/B00IRN4K2Q?tag=lifewire-onsite-prod-20&ascsubtag=2844527%7Cn8af8ce4e4e98444395054c3c64676a3708%7C) .

 New users get a free three-hour preview, then you must log in via your television subscription provider to continue listening. However, games broadcast on CBS do not require a paid television subscription.

* **CBS Sports app** : If you don’t subscribe to any television service, then download the free[ CBS Sports app](https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=com.handmark.sportcaster) . In addition to listening to the games broadcast by this network television station, you'll get the full schedule of which stations are broadcasting which games along with the full tournament schedule once it becomes available.

 FAQ

* How do I make a March Madness bracket?  
 Go to[ Play.NCAA.com](https://play.ncaa.com/) to make your own March Madness bracket.[ ESPN.com](https://fantasy.espn.com/tournament-challenge-bracket/) also has a March Madness bracket maker.
* Where can I watch March Madness on TV?  
 You can watch some of March Madness on your local CBS station. To catch every March Madness game, you'll need access to CBS, TBS, TNT, and truTV. CBS is available through[ Paramount+](https://www.lifewire.com/cbs-all-access-4155838) , and you can get TBS, TNT and truTV through cable replacement streaming services like Max and[ Sling TV](https://www.lifewire.com/sling-tv-4154536) .
* How do I listen to the Super Bowl on the radio?  
 You can[ listen to the Super Bowl on the radio](https://www.lifewire.com/listening-to-super-bowl-on-radio-2843103) via SiriusXM, Westwood One, TuneIn Radio, NFL Game Pass, ESPN Radio, or the NFL app.
