## Expert Instructions for Linking to Shared Network Storage on Your Mac
### What to Know

* Launch**Finder** and then select**Go** \>**Connect to Server** from the menu.
* Enter the path for the network drive and select**Connect** . Click**Connect** again to confirm.
* Drag the shares into the**Login Items** section of System Settings to auto-mount them after a reboot.

 This article explains how to set up a[mapped drive](https://www.lifewire.com/what-is-a-mapped-drive-2625932) on a Mac and how to reconnect to the shared folders automatically after a reboot. These directions are for Macs running macOS Sonoma, but they can easily be adapted for other versions of macOS.

## How to Auto-Mount a Network Drive on macOS

 To automatically connect to your favorite network drives after a reboot, add them to the Open at Login area of System Settings.

1. Click the **Apple logo** and then choose**System Settings** or, if you're using an older version of macOS,**System Preferences** .

2. Search for**Login Items** , or select it from the**General** menu.

 FAQ

* Can I map a Windows network drive to my Mac?  
 Yes, sharing files between a Windows machine and your Mac is possible by[using OneDrive](https://www.lifewire.com/set-up-microsoft-onedrive-for-mac-2260846) , or with a[properly formatted](https://www.lifewire.com/format-usb-drive-in-fat32-on-windows-10-5113286) external or USB flash drive, Setting up[file sharing](https://www.lifewire.com/set-up-macs-file-sharing-options-2260207) on your Mac, or using[Windows file sharing](https://www.lifewire.com/network-file-sharing-in-microsoft-windows-818219) will also work.
* How do I map a network drive to my Mac using AFP?  
 From the Finder menu bar select**Go** \>**Connect to Server** \> then enter "afp://" followed by the IP address of the drive and select**Connect** . Choose a username and password for the drive when prompted.
